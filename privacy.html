Privacy policy

Effective date: 14 July 2026

Quran Within is designed to work without an account and to keep personal reflection data on your device.

Information stored on your device

The app stores reading progress, bookmarks, plans, reminder settings, and similar preferences on your device. Private reflections and du'a entries are stored in device-backed encrypted storage in installed iOS and Android apps. Ask history is session-only and is not saved as account history.

Information we do not collect

Quran Within does not require an account, sell personal information, use advertising SDKs, or provide cloud sync for your private reflections and du'a entries. The current public release does not enable a remote source-search service.

Network use

If you choose to stream recitation audio, your device connects to Quran Foundation's content delivery network. That provider may receive ordinary technical request information such as your IP address and device/network metadata needed to deliver the audio. Their handling is governed by their own policies.

Notifications and widgets

Optional daily reminders are scheduled locally on your device. Quran Within does not register a push-notification token or send reminders from a server. Widgets show public Quran content only; they do not show your private reflections or du'a entries.

Children

Quran Within is not directed to children under 13. Do not submit personal information through the support route.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the app's data practices change. The effective date at the top will be updated before a changed version is released.

Contact and privacy questions

For support or privacy questions, use the Quran Within support page. Do not post reflections, du'a text, account credentials, or other sensitive information in a public GitHub issue.
